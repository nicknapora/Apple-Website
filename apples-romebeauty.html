Rome Beauty

This specimen was procured at an orchard near my family's home, alongside the Arkansas Black and Pink Lady.

The Rome Beauty is widely considered an excellent cooking apple. I have reviewed it here as a raw eating apple. This is due in equal part to my efforts to give variety to the selections and to my own ignorance. I encourage you not to make the same mistake I did.

Taste: 1/5

The fact that it was effectively flavorless quickly proved to be the least of the Rome Beauty's problems as an eating apple.

Texture 1/5

The Rome Beauty specimen I procured, pictured middle, had the worst texture of any of the apples I have reviewed by an extreme margin. Beyond unpleasant, the flesh of the Rome Beauty seemed to dissolve in the mouth like toilet paper. As the flesh of the apple disintegrates, a relatively tough and leathery skin remains and quickly dominates the mouth.

Crispness 1/5

There is no reprieve from this swift collapse into coarse mush. It is arguably worse in this category than texture alone.

Juiciness 1/5

This is almost a non-applicable assessment. The specimen was so devoid of structure that juice instantly became indiscernable from mush.

Nick Napora 2021
