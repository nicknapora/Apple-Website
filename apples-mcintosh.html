McIntosh

Computer scientist Jef Raskin, first conceived and named the project that would become the Macintosh personal computer in the late 1970s, naming it for his favorite eating apple. The Macintosh diverged considerably from his original vision of the mass-market appliance-like computer, but his mark endured following his departure from Apple Computer in 1982. Does the eponym of the Macintosh live up?

Taste: 4/5

The flavor of the McIntosh was among the best of the samples I've reviewed. With a slight earthy note from its pleasantly hearty skin and noticeable tartness that I greatly enjoy, the McIntosh was pretty satisfying despite its texture.

Texture 1/5

While not quite as abysmal as that of the Rome Beauty, the McIntosh still had a very mealy flesh.

Crispness 3/5

This specimen was redeemed somewhat by its crunch. Despite its texture, it was adequately hard and its outer bites were accordingly satisfying.

Juiciness 2/5

The juice content of the McIntosh was unremarkable.

Nick Napora 2021
