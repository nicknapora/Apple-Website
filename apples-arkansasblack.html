Arkansas Black

This specimen was procured at an orchard near my family's home, alongside the Pink Lady and Rome Beauty.

Taste: 1/5

I am tempted to give the Arkansas Black a higher score for flavor simply because it is so unique. It has an almost bitter, earthy note, not tasting like any of the other varieties I have yet tried. Unfortunately, it is not especially pleasant, and I did not enjoy it.

Texture 2/5

The Arkansas Black, pictured left, was quite mealy, though not as bad as some other varieties reviewed here. Like most of the specimens I have reviewed, this sample was taken out of season, perhaps in part explaining its textural deficiencies.

Crispness 3/5

While the flesh was soft and mealy, the specimen was surprisingly hard. While most of this structure was provided by its relatively tough skin, the snappiness of the outer bites elevated the experience of eating this apple.

Juiciness 3/5

The Arkansas Black was fairly juicy given its texture, but even this aspect of the variety was marred by the unusual taste and poor texture of the specimen.

Nick Napora 2021
